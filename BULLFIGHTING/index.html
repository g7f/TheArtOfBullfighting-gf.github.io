The Art of Bullfighting


Bullfighting is a sacrigicial ritual un which men (and also a few women) pit themselves against an animal bred for the ring. In this "authentic religious drama", as poet Garica Lorca describe it, the spectator exercise vicariously the fear and felt by the matador. Althrough some Spaniards oppose it on grounds of its cruelty, it is as popular as ever. Many Spaniards see talk banning bullfighting as striking at the essence of their being, for they regard the toreo, the art of bulllfgiting, as a noble part of their heritage. Bullfighting today, however, are often debase by practices which weaken the bull, eapecially shaving its horns to make them blunt.

The toro bravo


Well treated at ranch, the toro bravo (fighting bull) is specially bred for qualities of aggressivtenes and courage. As aficionados of bullfighting point out in its defence, the young bull enjoy a full life while it is being prepared for its 15 minutes in thr ring. Bulls must be at least four years old before they fight.


Traje de luces


The matador wears a Traje de luces (suit of light), a colourful silk outfit embroidered with gold sequins.


Muleta


The passes are made with a muleta, a scarlet cape stiffened along one side.


Banderillas


Banderillas, barbed darts, are thrust into the bull's already weakened back muscles.
