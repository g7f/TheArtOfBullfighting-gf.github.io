The Bullfight

The corrida (bullfight) has three stages called tercios. In the first one, the tercio de varas, the matador and picadores (horsemen with lances) are aided by peones (assistants). In the terico de banderillos, banderillos stick pairs of darts in the bull's back. In the tercio the muleta the matador make a series of passes at the bull with a muleta (cape). He then execute the kill,the estocada, with a sword.



The matador plays the bull with a capa (red cape) in the tarcio the varas. Peones will then draw the bull towards the picadores.



Picadores goad the bull with steel-pointed lances, testing its bravery. The lances weaken the animal's shoulder muscles



Banderilleros enter to provoke the wounded bull in trcio de banderillas, sticking pairs of banderillas in its back.



The matador makes passes with the cape in the tercio de muleta, then lower it and thrusts in the sword for the kill.



The estocada recibiendo is a difficult kill, rarely seen. The matador awaits the bull's charge rather than moving to meet it.
